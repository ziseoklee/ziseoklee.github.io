The Ideal

The ideal is a distraction
to the real

Depth is not trivial
It takes time to reveal

Each layer you peel
And the fruits they each yield
Are all real and
You know this because
It is what you feel

The deeper the foundation
The higher the building
But Immediate transformations
Are what ideals are instilling

It's not how life works
I understand why you whine
But good things take time
And one day you'll shine

You can have it for real
But first have the will
to release the distraction
- the distraction of your ideal

The poem was me telling myself not to sacrifice the real and present for some hypothetical future. And the “ideal” goal for me is depth: Depth of understanding - in math and science and also in life, depth of trust, deep authentic relationships full of genuine interactions with another individual. But I wanted to express that what is real is not an absolute value of depth (like math becomes real after you get a phd or a relationship is real only within a committed marriage) but the deepening of such aspects, getting to know a field of study or another individual better. And the ‘peeling each layer’ was my way of preserving the rhyme while conveying this idea. I felt that each layer I peel off is a fruitful, authentic process and although immediately I might not notice any differences, I feel that I have progressed and grown.

Emission

One giant mountain stood erect upfront
Whose shadow darkened my path
Waves of exhaustion swept away passion
Leaving fear and inertia to confront

I took a step forward, but then I was lost
Before descending any deeper, I weighed its cost
This journey forward and upward
Could very well hurt me or worse - kill me

In the moment of overwhelming chaos, of collapse,
As I was succumbing to despair and hell,
Sun had reached the other side of the snowcap
Her shine had come with something to tell

"The mountain is not your tragedy but your triumph
The forest is your opportunity, not a nuisance
The shadow blinded you, but my shine found you
Now look above. You need only to aim at one star"

The Foolish traveler had not looked up
The guide is ever-present, high in the sky - a star
Toward that star, the traveler moved
At last, he saw green, ripe land, stretching afar

You are the Photon to my Electron. This is a poem dedicated to Yena, but not restricted to her only
