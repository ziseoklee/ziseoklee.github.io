    THE DECISION

    If I have to fall 2000 times to learn to stand, I will get back up 2001 times and more.

    The fundamental choice is, am I on my side?
    Am I on the side of legitimate struggle? Of acceptance, truth, reality, honesty, effort, vulnerability, growth, trasncendence, healing, triumph, empathy, attention, faith, commitment, grace, gratitude, regulation, forgiveness, love, peace, generosity, humility, and courage?
    It seems like the only other option is the path of insecurity, cowardice, ignorance, naivety, neediness, greed, deception, ungratefulness, victimization, defensiveness, condemnation, shame, guilt, judgement, reduction, and compartmentalization.

    Good things take time. Great successes are built on even greater failures with the addition of the courage and wisdom to continue. Early successes have a framing effect; it limits one's growth in the long run and creates dishonesty and a fear of failure. The right way to think about success is that the outcome is not the purpose, but a mere corollary of having fun and working my hardest to become my best.

    But seek first his kingdom and his righteousness, and all these things will be given to you as well. Therefore do not worry about tomorrow, for tomorrow will worry about itself. Each day has enough trouble of its own.

    THE FOOL PRECEDES THE MASTER In particular, neuroplasticity and muscle plasticity is the ability of the brain or muscle to alter its structural and functional properties in accordance with the environmental conditions imposed on it. The brain growth and muscle growth come from the experience of struggle. Research has recently shown something stunning-when students make a mistake in math, their brain grows, synapses fire, and connections are made; when they do the work correctly, there is no brain growth (Moser ret al. 2011)

    We should not view mistakes as learning failures, but as learning achievements. (Boaler 2013). Being outside our comfort zone is an extremely important place to be.

    REMEMBER

    Empty the mind, stay open to everything. I am a baby bamboo.

    REMEMBER my natural tendency to forget, repeat mistakes, and ignore God. I am a spiritual baby, forever dependent on the nurturing, loving, transforming force of God.

    REMEMBER loving myself requires the capacity to recognize that there is something about me that I need to work on. Loving others requires the capacity to recognize what the other person needs to work on.

    We need moments when we realize that we do not have it all together and that we are not perfect. If we deny that our behavior is inadequate and fail to seek ways to redeem ourselves by learning from what we have done wrong, then we are also denying ourself the opportunity for growth and self-love.

    REMEMBER how grateful I was to meet my limitations during my workout at ExtremeS. I couldn't do the back-pull exercise lying down, so I did it like the female exercisers - leaning diagonally. But it was still hard. The experience of meeting my limitations is unpleasant at first, but it gives me a purpose, direction, and it propells me to change. To live.

    REMEMBER what I did. In my darkest, most miserable moments, I know what I did instead of giving up. I had nobody anyway. There was no team. I built, tried again, and aimed up even when I had nothing and no one.

    EMOTION

    A glimpse into the wonders of the unconscious mind: Emotion is not a lower function localized in an ancestral primitive brain. It is a large-scale network property of the nervous system.

    Spend more time with the people you care about. Friends and family.
    We naturally take relationships for granted. Like regular workouts or study sessions, the people who made it a practice to stay connected to people they care about regularly were the ones who stayed connected and happy.

    Age 80 respondents, when asked, "What are you most proud of?" They all answered, "A good relationship" (nobody mentioned achievement)
    When asked about their biggest regret, they answered, "I wish I had spent more time with people I care about."

    The best emotional strategy is the strategy of emotional awareness. If I know myself better than any other person, I am less prone to being affected or hurt by others' response to my thoughts, motives, or emotions. Discern the validity of the emotion - whether or not the emotion is based on a delusion. If it is, expressing the emotion will cause confusion and creep out the other person.

    Everyone goes through suffering and tragedy, stress and fear. They are not an indicator of a bad life nor a good life. A measure of wellness of life might be quality of relationships, or more fundamentally, a life lived to its full potential given all the tragedies and obstacles and gifts and opportunities.

    I have the power to give my life its meaning by maintaining integrity, responsibility, honesty, discipline, and by aiming at God (even in ways that seem embarrassingly trivial) despite all the reasons not to. I can choose the meaning of my suffering instead of passively conforming to societal standards.
