Five Qualities of Me

of which I am Proud

  1. I am open to the truth despite how painful it may be.
    Curiosity outweighs the pain and struggle. I often design and implement experiments to answer my questions.
  2. I am brave in the sense that I am willing to be bad at something to get better at it.
    The prospect of short-term failure and mistakes do not keep me from attempting to learn from them.
  3. I do not give up easily.
    I believe problems do not just go away, so I work through them instead of avoiding them.
  4. I am very self-reflective.
    I reflect consistently to stay aware of my own emotions, vulnerabilities, beliefs, and presumptions.
  5. I am a genuine listener and conversationalist.
    I am equipped with the appropriate language for deep communication and empathy. Try me.

Five Qualities of Me

of which I am not Proud

  1. I am susceptible to shame, self-disgust, and self-contempt.
    I suffer very high levels of shame and guilt even in situations that occur through no fault of my own.
  2. I am a sore loser.
    I get very upset, frustrated, and self-critical very easily. I need to fight this negative cycle when it occurs.
  3. I do not let go easily.
    I need a long time to accept that some phenomena are just incomprehensible and move on with my life.
  4. I struggle with self-discipline.
    I wish I could wake up, work out, and go to the library at the same time like a habit, but it's always a struggle.
  5. I am over-concerned with certain things.
    I find myself bothered by details, morality, hygiene, and achievement way more than I should be.
